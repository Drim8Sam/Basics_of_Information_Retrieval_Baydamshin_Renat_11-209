<p><b>Shawarma</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ʃ/: &#39;sh&#39; in &#39;shy&#39;">ʃ</span><span title="/ə/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;w&#39; in &#39;wind&#39;">w</span><span title="/ɑːr/: &#39;ar&#39; in &#39;far&#39;">ɑːr</span><span title="&#39;m&#39; in &#39;my&#39;">m</span><span title="/ə/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl">شاورما</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Middle_Eastern_cuisine" title="Middle Eastern cuisine">Middle Eastern</a> dish that originated in the <a href="/wiki/Levant" title="Levant">Levant</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Marks&#95;2010&#95;1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marks_2010-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-Prichep&#95;2015&#95;3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Prichep_2015-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-Salloum&#95;2012&#95;4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Salloum_2012-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-Mattar2004&#95;5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mattar2004-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> consisting of meat that is cut into thin slices, stacked in an inverted cone, and roasted on a slow-turning <a href="/wiki/Rotisserie#Vertical_rotisserie" title="Rotisserie">vertical spit</a>. Traditionally made with lamb or mutton, it may also be made with chicken, turkey meat, beef, <a href="/wiki/Falafel" title="Falafel">falafel</a> or veal.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Albala&#95;2011&#95;6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albala_2011-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-Davidson&#95;2014&#95;7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Davidson_2014-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-Marks&#95;2010&#95;1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marks_2010-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The surface of the rotisserie meat is routinely shaved off once it cooks and is ready to be served.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Shawarma is a popular street food throughout the <a href="/wiki/Arab_world" title="Arab world">Arab world</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Middle_East" title="Greater Middle East">Greater Middle East</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-Street&#95;Food&#95;11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Street_Food-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-:0&#95;12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-WalkerCarter2007&#95;13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WalkerCarter2007-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Etymology">Etymology</h2></div>
<p>The name <span title="Arabic-language romanization"><i lang="ar-Latn">shāwarmā</i></span> in <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> is a rendering of the term <span title="Ottoman Turkish (1500-1928)-language romanization"><i lang="ota-Latn">çevirme</i></span> in <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a> (<span class="IPA nowrap" lang="tr-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Turkish" title="Help:IPA/Turkish">&#91;tʃeviɾˈme&#93;</a></span>, <abbr style="font-size:85%" title="literal translation">lit.</abbr><span style="white-space: nowrap;">&#8201;</span><span class="gloss-quot">'</span><span class="gloss-text">turning; hence, roughly synonymous to döner in this context</span><span class="gloss-quot">'</span>), referring to <a href="/wiki/Rotisserie" title="Rotisserie">rotisserie</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-:0&#95;12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2></div>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Restauranthouder_bij_zijn_shoarma_spit,_Bestanddeelnr_255-6509.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Restauranthouder_bij_zijn_shoarma_spit%2C_Bestanddeelnr_255-6509.jpg/250px-Restauranthouder_bij_zijn_shoarma_spit%2C_Bestanddeelnr_255-6509.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="252" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Restauranthouder_bij_zijn_shoarma_spit%2C_Bestanddeelnr_255-6509.jpg/500px-Restauranthouder_bij_zijn_shoarma_spit%2C_Bestanddeelnr_255-6509.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="2614" /></a><figcaption>Shawarma preparation in <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>, 1950</figcaption></figure>
<p>The shawarma technique&#8212;grilling a vertical stack of <a href="/wiki/Meat" title="Meat">meat</a> slices and cutting it off as it cooks&#8212;first appeared in the Ottoman Empire in the 19th century in the form of <a href="/wiki/Doner_kebab" title="Doner kebab">döner kebab</a>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Marks&#95;2010&#95;1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marks_2010-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-Zeit&#95;1996-05-10&#95;14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zeit_1996-05-10-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> which both the Greek <a href="/wiki/Gyros" title="Gyros">gyros</a> and the Levantine shawarma are derived from.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Marks&#95;2010&#95;1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marks_2010-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-Prichep&#95;2015&#95;3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Prichep_2015-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-kremezi&#95;16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kremezi-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Author <a href="/w/index.php?title=Socrates_Spiro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Socrates Spiro (page does not exist)">Socrates Spiro</a>'s 1895 Egyptian Arabic-English dictionary translated <i>šāwirma</i> (<span title="Arabic-language text"><span lang="ar" dir="rtl">شاورمه</span></span>) as "rich stew, roast meat".<sup id="cite&#95;ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Shawarma led to the development during the early 20th century of the contemporary Mexican dish <i><a href="/wiki/Al_pastor" title="Al pastor">tacos al pastor</a></i> when it was brought there by <a href="/wiki/Lebanese_Mexicans" title="Lebanese Mexicans">Lebanese immigrants</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Prichep&#95;2015&#95;3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Prichep_2015-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The dish is also especially popular in <a href="/wiki/Ottawa" title="Ottawa">Ottawa, Ontario</a>, where a large community of the Lebanese diaspora exists.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-ottawalaheeb&#95;19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ottawalaheeb-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Preparation">Preparation</h2></div>
<p>Shawarma is prepared from thin cuts of seasoned and marinated lamb, mutton, veal, beef, chicken, or turkey. The slices are stacked on a skewer<sup id="cite&#95;ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> about 60&#160;cm (20&#160;in) high. Pieces of fat may be added to the stack to provide extra juiciness and flavour. A motorized spit slowly turns the stack of meat in front of an electric or gas-fired heating element, continuously roasting the outer layer. Shavings are cut off the rotating stack for serving, customarily with a long, flat knife.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Marks&#95;2010&#95;1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marks_2010-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Spices may include <a href="/wiki/Cumin" title="Cumin">cumin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cardamom" title="Cardamom">cardamom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cinnamon" title="Cinnamon">cinnamon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turmeric" title="Turmeric">turmeric</a> or <a href="/wiki/Paprika" title="Paprika">paprika</a>, and in some areas <i><a href="/wiki/Baharat" title="Baharat">baharat</a></i>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-haaretz2017&#95;21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-haaretz2017-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-Salloum&#95;2012&#95;4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Salloum_2012-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Shawarma is commonly served as a sandwich or wrap, in a flatbread such as <a href="/wiki/Pita" title="Pita">pita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Markook_(bread)" class="mw-redirect" title="Markook (bread)">shrak</a> (saj), <a href="/wiki/Laffa" title="Laffa">laffa</a> or <a href="/wiki/Lavash" title="Lavash">lavash</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Marks&#95;2010&#95;1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marks_2010-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-ottawalaheeb&#95;19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ottawalaheeb-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In the Middle East, chicken shawarma is typically served with <a href="/wiki/Garlic_sauce" title="Garlic sauce">garlic sauce</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_fries" title="French fries">fries</a>, and pickles. The garlic sauce served with the sandwich depends on the meat. <i><a href="/wiki/Toum" title="Toum">Toum</a></i> or <i>toumie</i> sauce is made from garlic, vegetable oil, lemon, and egg white or starch, and is usually served with chicken shawarma. <i><a href="/wiki/Tarator_(sauce)" title="Tarator (sauce)">Tarator</a></i> sauce is made from garlic, tahini sauce, lemon, and water, and is served with beef shawarma.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, most shawarma is made with dark-meat turkey, commonly served with <a href="/wiki/Tahina" class="mw-redirect" title="Tahina">tahina</a> sauce instead of yogurt for <a href="/wiki/Kashrut" title="Kashrut">kashrut</a> reasons.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-haaretz2017&#95;21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-haaretz2017-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is often garnished with diced tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, pickled vegetables, <a href="/wiki/Hummus" title="Hummus">hummus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garlic_mayonnaise" class="mw-redirect" title="Garlic mayonnaise">garlic mayo</a>, tahini sauce, <a href="/wiki/Sumac" title="Sumac">sumac</a>, or <i><a href="/wiki/Amba_(condiment)" title="Amba (condiment)">amba</a></i> mango sauce.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Marks&#95;2010&#95;1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marks_2010-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some restaurants offer additional toppings, including grilled peppers, eggplant, or French fries.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-haaretz2019&#95;24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-haaretz2019-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> 
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>, shawarma is traditionally made with thin cuts of marinated meat which is left marinating overnight in spices such as coriander, cumin, cardamom, paprika, garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Some forgo the vertical spit in favor of a more traditional horizontal spit; this allows cooking over hot coals rather than electrical heaters, similar to <a href="/wiki/Ca%C4%9F_kebab%C4%B1" title="Cağ kebabı">cağ kebabı</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Nigerian Sharwama is a staple streetfood in <a href="/wiki/Lagos_State" title="Lagos State">Lagos</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> the veggies are different as typically there are no tomatoes or onions in most Nigerian style sharwama but rather shredded cabbage and sometimes shredded carrots too.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Nigerian shawarma uses spices like garlic, thyme, cumin, onion power, black pepper, paprika as well as boullion cubes.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Hot dog or sausage is also added to the sharwama. The sauce is a creamy blend of salad cream, mayo and ketchup with pepper powder.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> There are three main variants, chicken sharwama, beef sharwama and a combo version of both meat.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
